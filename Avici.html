$AVICI Token Launch: Built with Futarchy

Here's something you don't see every day: $AVICI launched through MetaDAO using Futarchy. Instead of the usual token sale where early insiders get sweet deals, Futarchy uses prediction markets. People literally bet money on whether proposals will make the token more valuable.

It took Ram and the team months to build this infrastructure ‚Äì gas abstractions, bridges, full card infrastructure with self-custody. The UX is exceptional: you can deposit any token (thousands supported) and swap to your card balance with minimal slippage.

What is MetaDAO & Futarchy?

MetaDAO is the first project to actually implement Futarchy in the real world. It's a governance system invented by economist Robin Hanson back in 2000, but nobody had the balls to try it until now.

Here's how it works: Instead of voting on proposals, you trade in prediction markets. If you think a proposal will make the token price go up, you buy. If you think it'll tank the price, you sell. The market with higher predicted value wins.

Why does this matter? Because it's way harder to manipulate than regular voting. Whales can't just vote themselves free money ‚Äì they'd have to convince the entire market that their proposal is actually good for the token price. And markets are surprisingly good at sniffing out bullshit.
